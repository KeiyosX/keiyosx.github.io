Journey

Born in 1998, I was a little fat baby but due to medical complications, I lost a lot of weight. I went to Ashmole Primary School. I was no popular kid but I got by as your normal average everyday nerd who obsessed over video games (Sonic the Hedgehog in particular). From this age, I already knew what I wanted to become: I was going to become someone who can and is going to create awesome video games for people to enjoy.

Then I went to Lambeth Academy in Clapham for Secondary school. It was at this point where things got a lot more difficult. With no friends from primary school going there as well. I was pretty much alone for Y7 and Y8. Being a nerd, this means I got bullied a lot too but I never let it get to me (I'm just gonna creep) as I still had my end goal in mind and wanted to strive towards it. Around year 10 my work ethic strated to slip since... I dunno, puberty? While My A Grade GCSE Maths Grade was achieved in Year 10, this was my only A grade. I got mostly C's which I'm not mad about. However, this is the time my self esteem began to improve as I shrugged of my troubles and tried to enjoy each day as it came; tommorow's problems were for tommorow's me. I also made a proper group of friends instead of only talking to people in class and being a loner and break and lunch time.

A level's though? Year 12 and 13 was great in terms of hanging out with friends but school work? It was not the one. A2 did go better in terms of maths, I got a B in AS maths and D* in Business sub Diploma. Despite these grades, I don't struggle with explaining any of the course content so it's not a true reflection of my ability. I also begin to see teachers in a different way from before. As the class sizes were much smaller, they became more like mentors who actually wanted you to suceed. That was nice.

During my time in sixth form, I'd heard about the Step Forward program which caught my attention when I heard there'd be a 9K salary (and also work experience there may have been something about that). I successfully applied and now I'm a Service Desk Analyst at Tandem Bank. Everyone there is pretty chill and the work atmosphere and culture is always lively.
